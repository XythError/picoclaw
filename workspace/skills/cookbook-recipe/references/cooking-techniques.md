# Cooking Techniques Reference

## Knife Skills & Prep

- **Dice**: Uniform cubes. Small: ~6mm, Medium: ~12mm, Large: ~20mm
- **Mince**: Very fine chop (garlic, herbs)
- **Chiffonade**: Stack leaves, roll, slice into thin ribbons (basil, spinach)
- **Julienne**: Thin matchstick strips (~3mm × 3mm × 5cm)
- **Brunoise**: Very fine dice (~3mm) from julienne strips

## Common Cooking Methods

### Sauté
High heat, small amount of fat, constant motion. Best for tender vegetables, small pieces of meat. Pan must be hot before adding oil.

### Roasting
Dry heat in oven (180–230°C / 350–450°F). Great for vegetables, whole poultry, large cuts. Use a rack to allow air circulation.

### Braising
Brown first, then cook low-and-slow in liquid (covered). Transforms tough cuts (chuck, short ribs, lamb shank) into tender results. 150–165°C (300–325°F) for 2–4 hours.

### Blanching
Brief boil (1–3 min) followed by ice bath. Preserves color and texture in vegetables. Used before freezing.

### Poaching
Gentle cook in barely simmering liquid (70–80°C / 160–180°F). For eggs, fish, chicken breasts. Liquid should tremble, not boil.

### Steaming
Cook over (not in) boiling water. Preserves nutrients. 100°C. Good for fish, dumplings, vegetables.

## Baking Fundamentals

### Leavening Agents
- **Baking soda** (sodium bicarbonate): Needs an acid (buttermilk, yogurt, citrus). Reacts immediately—bake right away.
- **Baking powder**: Contains acid + base. Double-acting: reacts twice (mixing + heat).
- **Yeast**: Biological. Produces CO₂ over time. Needs warmth (24–32°C / 75–90°F), not hot (kills at >49°C / 120°F).

### Gluten Development
- **More gluten** (chewy bread): Bread flour (high protein ~13%), longer kneading, less fat
- **Less gluten** (tender cake): Cake flour (low protein ~8%), minimal mixing, fat coats flour strands

### Hydration
Bread dough hydration = water weight / flour weight × 100
- ~65%: Average sandwich loaf
- ~75%: Ciabatta, focaccia
- ~80%+: Open crumb artisan loaf (requires stretch-and-fold technique)

## Meat Temperature Guide (Internal)

| Meat | Done | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Beef steak – rare | 52°C / 125°F | Cool red center |
| Beef steak – medium-rare | 57°C / 135°F | Warm red center |
| Beef steak – medium | 63°C / 145°F | Warm pink center |
| Beef steak – well done | 71°C / 160°F | Little pink |
| Ground beef | 71°C / 160°F | Food safety minimum |
| Pork chops | 63°C / 145°F | Rest 3 min |
| Chicken breast | 74°C / 165°F | No pink |
| Chicken thigh | 79°C / 175°F | Better texture |
| Lamb – medium | 63°C / 145°F | |
| Fish (most) | 63°C / 145°F | Flakes easily |
| Salmon | 52°C / 125°F | Medium (silky) |

Always rest meat after cooking: 5 min for steaks, 10–15 min for roasts.

## Pan Heat Guide

| Heat Level | Temperature | Use For |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Low | 120–150°C | Melting, warming, slow simmer |
| Medium-low | 150–175°C | Sweat aromatics, gentle sauté |
| Medium | 175–200°C | Most sautéing, pan sauces |
| Medium-high | 200–230°C | Searing, stir-fry |
| High | 230°C+ | Quick sear, wok cooking |

## Common Sauce Building

1. **Fond** (brown bits): After searing, leave them in the pan
2. **Aromatics**: Shallots or garlic, medium heat until soft
3. **Deglaze**: Wine, stock, or acid to lift the fond
4. **Reduce**: Simmer until thickened
5. **Mount**: Whisk in cold butter off heat for gloss and richness
6. **Season**: Salt, pepper, lemon juice to taste
